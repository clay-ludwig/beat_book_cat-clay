## Talbot County Beat Book - January 11, 2026

**Story 1: 13th annual Watermen’s Appreciation Day draws a crowd**

*   The 13th annual Watermen’s Appreciation Day was held at the Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum on August 12, 2024.
*   The event was started in 2010 by association president Jeff Harrison.
*   The museum benefits from admission intake and partners with watermen on fundraising efforts.
*   Features included boat docking contests, steamed crabs, live music, and family activities.
*   Volunteers Ashley and James Harrison have been volunteering since age 10 with their children.

**Story 2: Interim executive director named for Chesapeake Music**

*   Margaret Enloe-North was named Interim Executive Director of Chesapeake Music in September 2023.
*   She brings 30 years of nonprofit experience to the role.
*   Chesapeake Music has been hosting concerts since its founding over 35 years ago.

**Story 4: Community celebrates Earth Day at Adkins Arboretum**

*   Adkins Arboretum celebrated Earth Day on April 21, 2024, with 300 attendees.
*   Environmental groups and government agencies hosted information tables.
*   Master gardeners and monarch butterfly experts were present.
*   Attendees received Eastern Redbud saplings to plant at home.
*   Ginny Tiernan, executive director of Adkins Arboretum, stated that Earth Day heightens awareness and appreciation for nature.
*   Jay Falstad emphasized the importance of protecting the planet.

**Story 5: Talbot County considering new cannabis legislation**

*   Talbot County is reviewing regulations on cannabis operations.
*   Drafted legislation proposes a 500-foot setback from residential properties.
*   Two state license holders, Dave Ferraris and Lori Yates, expressed concerns over the restrictions during a public hearing on July 8.
*   The county's planning and zoning director noted that a 200-foot setback is possible if affected property owners agree.
*   The legislation requires processors and growers to operate on at least 20-acre lots and procure professional testimony on cannabis odor.

**Story 7: Massaro leads Talbot in opener**

*   Anthony Massaro led Talbot Post 70/77 to a 4-3 win over Queen Anne’s-Kent County on July 16, 2024, in Game 1 of their American Legion Eastern Shore League North Division playoff series.

**Story 8: Ruth Candace Prossner**

*   Ruth Candace Prossner, age 79, of Royal Oak, MD, passed away on August 5, 2023, due to complications of Parkinson’s disease.
*   She was born in Utica, NY on July 26, 1944.
*   She is survived by her husband David and four sons, eight grandchildren, and predeceased all five siblings.
*   A private ceremony will be held with her family; memorial donations may be sent to the Talbot County Hospice.

**Story 10: Department of Aging funding anticipated to remain flat, secretary says**

*   Maryland Secretary of Aging Carmel Roques announced that funding for the Maryland Department of Aging is expected to remain flat over the coming year.
*   The department's budget will not be cut but will also not grow.
*   The federal Administration on Community Living recently changed regulations to improve access to services.
*   Efforts are focused on supporting older adults and their families.
