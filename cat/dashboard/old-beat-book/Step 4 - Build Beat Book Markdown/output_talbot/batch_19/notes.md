## Talbot County Beat Book - January 11, 2026

**Story 1: County council approves change to alcohol code allowing for possible liquor store in Trappe**

*   The Talbot County Council approved a change to the county’s alcoholic beverage code on January 18, 2024.
*   The change enables one Class E establishment in any municipality, including Trappe.
*   Trappe currently does not have this license.
*   The bill aimed to provide residents with access to a local liquor store, as they currently have to drive to Easton or Cambridge.

**Story 2: Talbot County approves senior task force**

*   Talbot County Council passed a resolution to create a Senior Citizens Task Force on August 13, 2025.
*   The task force will make recommendations on housing, healthcare, transportation, and meal services for homeless seniors.
*   Representatives from Commission on Aging, Delmarva Community Services, Talbot Interfaith Shelter, and other entities will be part of the task force.
*   Council member Lynn Mielke proposed the task force earlier this summer.

**Story 4: Police Unity Tour peddles through Denton**

*   The event raises awareness about fallen law enforcement officers and money for the National Law Enforcement Officers Memorial.
