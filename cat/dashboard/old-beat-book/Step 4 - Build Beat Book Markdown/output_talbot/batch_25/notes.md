## Talbot County Reporting Notes - January 11, 2026

**General Events & Culture:**

*   “Sarah Drew and a guide dog star in new Hallmark Movies & Mysteries film” - Sarah Drew starred in “Guiding Emily,” a Hallmark Movies & Mysteries film released on Sept. 8, marking National Dog Service Month. The film features Antonio Cupo and Sharon Taylor. Drew visited the Guide Dogs of America open house before filming.
*   Temple B’nai Israel presents ‘a joyous evening of klezmer music’ – The Seth Kibel Trio will perform on March 6 at the Satell Center for Jewish Life on the Eastern Shore. The program includes klezmer music, Yiddish theater, Yiddish folksong, jazz, and swing music. Admission is free and can be registered at bnaiisraeleaston.org/event/Klezmer or by calling 410-822-0553.
*   Julianne Moore’s career began on soap operas “The Edge of Night” and “As the World Turns,” earning a Daytime Emmy Award for her work from 1985 to 1988. She later appeared in TV miniseries, movies, and played Sarah Palin in the 2012 HBO project “Game Change,” winning a Primetime Emmy Award for her role.
*   Peter Panyon and his band Big Tribe will present “Songs About the Water” on September 28 at the Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum’s Van Lennep Auditorium. The concert features songs inspired by local rivers, including “Can’t Work the River,” which tells a waterman’s struggles with the Patuxent River.
*   The Academy Art Museum in Easton will host its annual Juneteenth celebration on June 22 from noon to 4 p.m. The event is a collaboration with BAAM, Frederick Douglass Honor Society, and Talbot County Free Library.
*   Anita Groener’s art exhibit “To the Edge of Your World” at the Academy Art Museum in Easton, MD, uses small-scale items to explore themes such as war and homelessness. The exhibit features grid-like drawings and bird-chirping sounds, including two sculpture works titled Cradle and Shrapnel made from twigs collected over 10 years.
*   The Chesapeake Film Festival showcased “Lives Beyond Motion” by Keith Glassman and “100 Foot Photo” by Matt Kresling at the Academy Art Museum in Easton, MD.
*   John North died on September 19, 2025, at age 94. He was a well-respected judge who also served as Talbot County state’s attorney and chairman of the Maryland Critical Area Commission. North played a crucial role in protecting the Chesapeake Bay’s health and implementing local critical area programs.
*   Colonel Richardson High School will dedicate its basketball court to former coach Merrill Morgan on Thursday at 5 p.m. Morgan coached for 38 seasons, from an unspecified start date to August 7, 2022, when he passed away at age 84 due to Parkinson’s Disease.
*   Sam Murphy hit a game-winning basket with 12.8 seconds remaining, giving Saints Peter and Paul High a 42-39 victory over Easton on December 20, 2023. Morgan Quade scored a game-high 17 points, and Lucy Rankin sealed the win with a layup after Sam Murphy’s drive-to-the-hoop shot.
*   Lynda Balslev’s recipe for Tomato, Arugula and Orzo Salad features fresh summer ingredients including cherry tomatoes, heirlooms, and Early Girls, combined with dainty orzo, mozzarella, Parmigiano-Reggiano, arugula, Kalamata olives, basil, and balsamic vinegar.

**Government & Local Issues:**

*   The Talbot Board of Education added a makeup day to this year’s school calendar due to snow and approved a revised 180-day requirement. The board unanimously passed a new calendar for the 2025-26 school year with three options: two days off in March, five days for spring break, or an eight-day spring break. The final calendar was adopted with a 6-1 vote, featuring two inclement weather days in March and a five-day spring break.
*   Talbot County removed its diversity, equity and inclusion statement from the employee handbook amid concerns over millions of dollars in federal funding for an ongoing airport project. Council members voted 4-1 to remove the statement, with Keasha Haythe opposing. The resolution also voids two previous diversity resolutions and requests a replacement welcoming statement.
*   Gov. Wes Moore emphasized that solving the budget crisis is an "enormous task" made difficult by federal constraints.
*   The Maryland General Assembly continues to discuss the budget bill, with representatives from Talbot and Caroline Counties fighting for relief from potential cost shifts.
*   The Trappe Post Office has reopened after a two-year renovation period that began in October 2022. The post office had been closed to the public, causing residents with post office boxes to travel to Cambridge for mail pickup. Postal clerk Jasmine is returning home from working in Cambridge during the renovation.

**Financial & Economic:**

*   Talbot County Council sent a letter to the Maryland General Assembly expressing concerns over $1 million in county money impacted by the state’s Budget Reconciliation and Financing Act.
*   Dorchester County may be looking at more than $3 million affected, while Caroline County may lose $1.1 million in income tax revenue if tax cut plans are approved.
