**STORY 5: CASA is grateful for its volunteers this holiday season**
*   CASA of the Mid-Shore advocates for children in Talbot, Dorchester, Queen Anne’s, and Kent circuit courts due to abuse, neglect, or abandonment.
*   CASA volunteers, at least 21 years old, have completed pre-service training.
*   Volunteers identify unique child needs, advocate for services, and provide stability and support.
*   The organization is grateful for volunteers, particularly during the holiday season, as they help children find hope amidst challenges.
*   National studies confirm the value of CASA – children are more hopeful when a CASA is present in their lives.

**STORY 6: 'Spirit of the Shore' CBMM celebrating Watermen’s Appreciation Day**
*   Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum (CBMM) is celebrating Watermen’s Appreciation Day in St. Michaels on August 11.
*   The event is 10am-4pm.
*   It honors men and women who work on the Chesapeake Bay, including harvesting crabs and oysters.
*   Features a boat docking contest, steamed crabs, live music, and kids activities.
*   CBMM is partnering with the Talbot Waterman Association.
*   Tickets are available online or at the door, with discounted pricing for members.

**STORY 7: History is filled with many 'what if' questions**
*   Historian Fred Kaplan argues that if President John Kennedy had not been assassinated in November 1963, the outcome and duration of the Vietnam War would have been different.
*   Kennedy's decision to accept a Soviet proposal to remove nuclear missiles from Cuba in exchange for removing American missiles from Turkey suggests he may have avoided escalation in Vietnam.
*   If Kennedy had lived, America might have avoided a futile and divisive war resulting in over 58,000 deaths and widespread PTSD among veterans.
