**Tench Tilghman: A Talbot County-born revolutionary hero:**

*   “Tench Tilghman was born in Talbot County, Maryland in 1744 and became one of George Washington’s most trusted men during the Revolutionary War.”
*   “He joined Washington’s staff in 1776 and played a critical role in delivering news of British surrender following the Battle of Yorktown in 1781.”
*   “Tilghman died in 1786 but is remembered for his leadership, hard work, and bravery.”
*   “A portrait of Tilghman was commissioned in 1781, depicting him and Marquis de Lafayette, which is displayed today in the Maryland State House.”
*   “In 2024, a local chapter of the Sons of the American Revolution honored Tilghman with Tench Tilghman Week from October 19-24.”
