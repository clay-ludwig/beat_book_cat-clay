**Talbot County Board of Education:**

*   The Talbot County Board of Education approved a new strategic plan on July 16, 2024.
*   Policy updates on employee code of conduct and video recording were introduced but not voted on.
*   The district is searching for a new retirement plan provider, with finalists to be interviewed in August.
*   Data from the 2024-2025 school year was reviewed, including test scores and climate surveys.

**Reproductive Freedom Amendment:**

*   Maryland voters will decide on Question 1, a constitutional amendment guaranteeing “reproductive freedom.”
*   The amendment enshrines the right to abortion and reproductive autonomy.
*   Gov. Wes Moore, Pro-Choice Maryland, and the Maryland State Education Association endorse the amendment.
*   The Maryland Catholic Conference and other conservative groups oppose the amendment.

**Chesapeake Bay Underwater Grass:**

*   Underwater grass populations in Maryland’s portion of the Chesapeake Bay decreased from 38,188 acres in 2023 to 36,794 acres in 2024.
*   The state’s restoration goal of 79,800 acres was not met.
*   Freshwater populations did well, with five rivers surpassing their goals.
*   Widgeon grass populations declined significantly, especially in Eastern Bay and Tangier Sound.
*   The loss of widgeon grass is concerning due to its sensitivity to changes in water quality.

**Realtor Safety:**

*   Local Realtors are taking precautions to protect themselves and clients during open houses and property showings.
*   Nationally, 4% of agents reported being victims of crime, with most incidents occurring at open houses and in their vehicles.
*   Realtors use visual recordings, posted signs, lock box systems like SentriLock, and take preventative measures such as taking license plate numbers and meeting clients in public locations.

**Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum:**

*   The Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum is celebrating its 60th year, opening in 1965 at Navy Point with two buildings and becoming accredited in 1978.
*   Pete Lesher, chief historian, said the museum was created by a community effort to preserve the region's history.
*   The museum now features exhibits such as Sailing to Freedom, which highlights the Underground Railroad on the water, and plans to honor African American business owners William Coulbourne and Frederick Jewett with its education building name change.
