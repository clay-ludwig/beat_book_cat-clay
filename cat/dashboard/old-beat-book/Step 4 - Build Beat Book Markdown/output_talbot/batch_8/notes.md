## Talbot County Reporting Notes - January 11, 2026

**STORY 1: Port Street housing project nearing completion**

*   The Port Street Commons housing project in Easton, MD, is nearing completion.
*   Nine affordable units are being built.
*   The project includes a new headquarters for The Arc Central Chesapeake Region.
*   Construction began in June 2022.
*   Expected completion date is October 2024.
*   Jonathon Rondeau, president and CEO of The Arc, aims to provide immediate assistance to those in need of housing in the Easton area.
*   Ribbon-cutting ceremony is planned for November 7.
*   First tenants moving in on December 1.

**STORY 2: Maryland House and Garden tour lets visitors explore Talbot County properties**

*   The Maryland House and Garden Pilgrimage tour benefits preservation and restoration efforts.
*   Canterbury Manor in Easton, MD, was visited during the tour.
*   Canterbury Manor features French copper pots, a Steinway Piano, and English furniture.
*   Proceeds go toward preserving Talbot County properties, including Scotts United Methodist Church and St. Paul’s Episcopal Church in Trappe.
*   Other properties visited included Ellenborough, Easton’s 219 S. Hanson St., Chloras Point Farm, Ferry Farm House, and Lloyds Landing.

**STORY 8: Pastoral Ponderings: The Lenten season**

*   Ash Wednesday marks the beginning of the Lenten season, a day associated with mortality and repentance.
*   The use of ashes as a symbol of penitence was common in early Christian ceremonies.
*   The word “Lent” comes from the Anglo-Saxon term for spring.
*   Ash Wednesday is an exception to the modern customs of Lent, as it retains its original meaning as a day of repentance.

**STORY 9: Talbot school board sees test scores, improvement initiatives**

*   State test scores released last month showed a slight increase in math and English proficiency levels at Talbot County Public Schools.
*   47.3% of students tested proficient in English and 18.3% in math.
*   The district is implementing initiatives, including an adaptive testing program and additional math coaches.
*   Experts will review the district’s progress, visiting three elementary schools this year, and the board is planning to replicate lessons learned at smaller schools at larger ones like Easton Elementary.

**STORY 10: 'Game-time decision': Clouds, storms could dim eclipse viewing in some parts of U.S.**

*   Some parts of the U.S., particularly Texas and Arkansas, could see cloudy skies and storms during Monday's total solar eclipse.
*   Over 4 million people are expected to travel for the event.
*   The most clear areas to view the eclipse in its totality path include northern parts of Arkansas, southeastern Missouri, southern Illinois, and southern Indiana.
*   Cloudy conditions are forecasted for Mid-Atlantic areas, including Easton and Ocean City, while central Texas may see clear skies.

**STORY 11: Planners not sold on 365-unit development near new Easton hospital**

*   Easton Sunrise Partners LLC and Scott Wagner are proposing a 365-unit residential development, Fox Chase Estates, on 112 acres off Airport Road near the Easton hospital.
*   The project aims to address affordable housing needs for healthcare workers.
*   Town officials have raised concerns about connectivity, density, and site plan issues.
*   A Planning Commission meeting ended with no positive recommendation.
*   The commission will revisit the proposal on July 17.
*   Approval would require a public hearing.
